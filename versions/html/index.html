A travel logbook from the crew deck

Notes from the Open Ocean

by Ramesh Rushantha Silva · Superyacht Bosun · Travel Vlogger · Water Sports Instructor · Musician

"From Narahenpita to the open ocean: twenty-five years of stage lights, ship decks and stories worth telling."

Born in Narahenpita, Sri Lanka, on 30 March 1983. Raised on schoolyard rugby at Isipathana College, sidetracked into Colombo's first ethnic fusion rock band, and finally pulled out to sea, where he has spent two decades working the decks of Europe's finest yachts.

The Logbook

Five chapters, so far

Chapter 01 · Narahenpita, 1983

Where the story starts

The eldest child of the family, raised on schoolyard rugby and cricket pitches. Under-13 through XV rugby at Isipathana College, three age-group cricket teams, and a sixteen-year-old selling books door to door before finding his bearing at Colombo International Nautical and Engineering College.

Isipathana CollegeRugbyCINEC

Chapter 02 · 1999 to 2010

Before the sea, the stage

Co-founder and percussionist of The Kreators, Sri Lanka's first ethnic fusion rock band. "Sri Lankan Mix" in 2000, the Animal Farm stage play in 2002, TNL Onstage finals, and after a reunion, the album Kreators Reborn 2010.

The KreatorsPercussionKreators Reborn

Chapter 03 · 2005 to today

Two decades on deck

Merchant navy first, then the superyachts: sixteen years aboard MY Amevi rising from deckhand to senior deck instructor, a bosun's promotion on the expedition yacht MY Brother Brancusi, and from 2025, a 96-metre private and charter yacht.

Chapter 04 · Walk With RAMA

Stories from the water

Travel and water sports, filmed where the work happens: the world's deepest dive pool in Dubai, dive sites off Koneshwaram, and a standing case that Sri Lanka's coasts belong on the world's water-sports map.

Chapter 05 · Back to shore

Ventures and advocacy

Co-founder of Universal Green Solutions, building waste-conversion machinery for a cleaner Sri Lanka. CEO of the Isipathana Online Information Center, a public voice for Henry Pedris Ground, and a champion of water sports tourism at home.

About the Author

The short version

Born 30 March 1983 in Narahenpita, Sri Lanka. Isipathana College, then Colombo International Nautical and Engineering College. Left home for the merchant navy in 2005 and has worked European superyachts since 2008: deckhand, deck instructor, senior deck instructor, and bosun.

Off the deck

Co-founded The Kreators in 1999, Sri Lanka's first ethnic fusion rock band, and Universal Green Solutions in 2022, developing waste-conversion machinery. Runs the Isipathana College Online Information Center and advocates for water sports as a growth engine for Sri Lankan tourism.

Why "Walk With RAMA"

Because the best stories are walked, sailed, dived and occasionally skied, and someone has to film them.
